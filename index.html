AI Autumn School


The AI Autumn School aims to foster knowledge transfer and capacity building in Artificial Intelligence for global healthcare. We aim to bridge the healthcare gap between Global North and Global South which often faces unique healthcare challenges that can be effectively addressed by deploying AI technologies.

EEDA focuses on addressing the challenges of implementing Artificial Intelligence (AI) in low-resource healthcare settings, particularly in low-middle-income countries (LMICs). It aims to achieve this through Affordable AI and Collaborative Federated Learning. The motivation stems from the pressing need to bridge the healthcare gap between resource-rich and resource-constrained regions. AI can significantly benefit LMICs, but barriers like limited resources and data availability hinder its adoption. Affordable AI and Collaborative Federated Learning offer promising solutions by optimizing computational requirements, ensuring data privacy, and enhancing affordability. By bringing together partners from Germany and Lebanon, EEDA aims to develop tailored AI courses for LMICs, focusing on Medical Imaging and Food Safety. These courses aim to transfer knowledge and build capacity in AI, ultimately improving medical diagnoses and global food safety practices in low-resource settings.
Python

Participants will acquire proficiency in Python, a widely used programming language in the field of artificial intelligence and machine learning

Math. & Statistics

Participants will develop a strong foundation in statistics and mathematics, which are essential for understanding and working with AI and machine learning algorithms

Analytical Thinking

The Autumn School will enhance participants’ analytical thinking skills, which are crucial for problem-solving in AI and machine learning
